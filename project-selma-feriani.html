Case Study 01 · Visual Identity

Selma Feriani Gallery

A visual identity system for a contemporary art gallery championing artists from North Africa and the Middle East — built to carry institutional authority while giving the art room to breathe.

Client

Selma Feriani Gallery

Sector

Cultural Institution

Role

Lead Graphic Designer

Deliverables

Identity, Editorial, Digital

Context & Brief

A gallery voice that matches the weight of its programme.

Selma Feriani Gallery presents major work by artists from North Africa and the Middle East — voices that deserve institutional space, weight, and elegance on the international stage. The brief: design a visual identity that could sit comfortably alongside London and Paris's most established contemporary galleries, while remaining legible across both physical exhibition spaces and digital platforms.

The challenge was restraint — building a system rigorous enough to feel institutional, but quiet enough that it never competes with the art itself.

Role & Responsibilities
  • — Developed the gallery's typographic hierarchy and editorial grid system, applied consistently across print and digital formats.
  • — Designed responsive desktop and mobile layouts for the gallery's online presence, prioritising large-format imagery of artworks.
  • — Established spacing and layout rules that scale from a single artwork page to a full exhibition catalogue.
  • — Presented and iterated the identity system directly with gallery leadership across multiple review rounds.
Creative Process

Restraint as respect.

Early exploration focused on typographic pairings that could feel both editorial and exhibition-ready — serif headlines for gravitas, paired with a clean grid that gives every artwork generous breathing room. Mobile-first wireframing ensured the gallery's growing international audience could browse exhibitions comfortably from a phone, a frequent overlooked detail in gallery websites of this scale.

Fig. 1–2 — Mobile layout exploration, prioritising artwork imagery over chrome.

Solution & Deliverables

A system built to scale.

The final identity centres on a flexible editorial grid, a refined serif/sans pairing, and a desktop experience that lets each artist's work take full visual precedence — exactly the kind of quiet institutional confidence the gallery's programme deserves.

Fig. 3 — Desktop homepage, full identity system applied.

Impact & Results

100%

Responsive across desktop, tablet and mobile — critical for an international, mobile-first audience.

2×

Layout templates built to scale from single-artwork pages to full exhibition catalogues without redesign.

∞

A flexible grid system designed to support the gallery's growing roster of exhibiting artists indefinitely.
