Case Study 04 · Art Direction & Motion

Kabéah

Hand-crafted stop-motion direction for a fragrance brand — four short films, each built around a bespoke tactile set designed to embody the character of its scent.

Client

Kabéah

Sector

Fragrance

Role

Art Direction & Stop-Motion

Deliverables

Set Design, 4 Motion Films

Context & Brief

Giving scent a visual texture.

Fragrance is one of the hardest products to communicate visually — it has no inherent image. For Kabéah, the brief was to give each of four fragrances its own short film, communicating the feeling of the scent through tactile, handmade visual language rather than conventional glossy product photography.

The result: four stop-motion films — Belle Épine, Secret de K, Nuit de Jasmin, and Lily Chérie — each with a fully designed, hand-built set matching its fragrance's mood.

Role & Responsibilities
  • — Designed and physically built four distinct miniature sets, each translating a fragrance's character into colour, texture and material.
  • — Directed and shot stop-motion animation frame-by-frame for each set, controlling lighting, pacing and composition by hand.
  • — Edited and sequenced each film to create a distinct sensory rhythm matching its fragrance's personality.
  • — Maintained a consistent tactile, handcrafted visual signature across all four films as a unifying brand language.
Creative Process

Built by hand, frame by frame.

Each set began with a single question: if this fragrance were a room, what would be in it? Belle Épine ("beautiful thorn") called for a set built around contrasts — soft petals against sharp, thorned forms. Nuit de Jasmin leaned into a nocturnal palette, with miniature jasmine-like forms lit to evoke a warm evening garden. Every set was constructed, lit and shot entirely by hand — embracing a tactile, single-take aesthetic rather than smoothing it out in post.

Fig. 1–2 — Stills from "Secret de K" and "Nuit de Jasmin," each set hand-built to match its fragrance's character.

Solution & Deliverables

Four films, one tactile signature.

The completed series — Belle Épine, Secret de K, Nuit de Jasmin, and Lily Chérie — gives Kabéah a recognisable motion identity built entirely from handcrafted, single-take stop-motion. The deliberate imperfection of the medium becomes the brand's signature: warm, intimate, and distinctly handmade in a category dominated by glossy CGI.

Fig. 3–4 — Stills from "Lily Chérie" and "Belle Épine," completing the four-film fragrance series.

Impact & Results

4

Hand-built sets and complete stop-motion films, each translating a distinct fragrance into a distinct visual world.

1:1

Direct, frame-by-frame control over lighting, pacing and composition — full ownership of the creative process from set design to final edit.

Tactile

A handcrafted motion signature that differentiates Kabéah from the glossy CGI language typical of the fragrance category.
