About

A designer who builds worlds, trained to do it properly.

I trained in art direction and graphic design at Istituto Marangoni, Paris — a foundation that taught me to treat every brand as a complete sensory universe, not a collection of assets. Since then, I've worked across cultural institutions, beauty and cosmetics brands, music identities, and food & beverage concepts, building visual systems that carry a brand's values into every touchpoint: logo, campaign, packaging, space, and screen.

Based in Tunis, with international training and a working knowledge of French, English, Arabic and Spanish, I'm currently building toward fluency in Japanese — a language I'm learning not as a checkbox, but because I want to understand the culture I'm designing for from the inside.

🌸
Philosophy

Innovation, within the codes.

My approach starts with restraint: understanding the existing visual language of a culture or category before deciding where to push against it. I believe the most memorable design doesn't reject convention outright — it understands convention deeply enough to know exactly where to bend it.

This is especially true working toward the Japanese market, where precision, seasonality, and the relationship between space and object carry meaning that a purely Western design education doesn't teach. I see this not as a constraint, but as a richer design language to learn — one I'm actively studying through Japanese aesthetic philosophy (間 — ma, the value of negative space; 物作り — monozukuri, the dignity of making) and applying to my own work.

Value Add for Japan

An outside perspective, held with care.

Working across Tunisia, Europe and international clients has given me a habit of designing for audiences whose visual language isn't my own — and of doing so respectfully, through research rather than assumption. For a Japanese team, I bring a multicultural eye that can identify what reads as fresh or surprising to a global audience, paired with the discipline to ask "does this actually fit here?" before presenting it.

I'm not arriving with a finished opinion about Japanese design — I'm arriving with genuine curiosity, a foundation in Japanese language and aesthetic philosophy, and the creative range to translate a brand's voice across cultural contexts without losing what makes it distinct. That combination — boldness balanced by listening — is, I believe, exactly the kind of outside-in perspective that strengthens a team rather than disrupting it.
