Case Study 02 · Brand Identity & Motion

House of Goddesses

A complete brand identity for an elevated beauty label — logotype, packaging, and motion graphics built around feminine power, ritual, and mythology.

Client

House of Goddesses

Sector

Beauty & Wellness

Role

Brand Designer & Art Director

Deliverables

Identity, Packaging, Motion

Context & Brief

A beauty brand between ceremony and contemporary luxury.

House of Goddesses needed a brand identity capable of carrying an ambitious positioning: beauty as ritual, rooted in mythology, but presented with the polish of a contemporary luxury label. The brief spanned the full identity system — logotype, motion language for digital campaigns, and packaging design — all of which needed to feel like a single coherent universe from first touch.

The core tension to resolve: how do you make something feel ancient and ceremonial, and simultaneously feel like an object someone would proudly display on a vanity in 2024?

Role & Responsibilities
  • — Designed the primary logotype and the "LAMB" monogram lockup, balancing organic curvature with precise geometric construction.
  • — Directed three short motion pieces translating the brand's identity into movement for digital and social platforms.
  • — Developed packaging concepts that carried the brand's mythological references without becoming literal or costume-like.
  • — Built a campaign-ready visual system spanning still imagery, motion, and product application.
Creative Process

Organic forms, precise type.

The starting point was the LAMB monogram — a wordmark constructed from interlocking organic letterforms that echo flowing hair and ritual gesture, set against the precision of a structured grid. This tension between fluid and exact became the visual rule for everything that followed: every motion piece, every packaging surface had to hold both qualities at once.
